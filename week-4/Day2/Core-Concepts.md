Video Game Studies Core Concepts Assignment

Section 1: History and Chronology

1. The Crash and The Revival
- Crash (1983): It happened because of too many bad games (like E.T.) and no quality control.
- Revival Company: Nintendo.
- Console: NES.
- Mascot: Mario.

2. Era Differentiation
- Golden Age (Arcades): Public focused. High scores and short games.
- Modern 3D & Online: Private (home) focused. Story and long online sessions.
- Examples: Pac-Man vs. World of Warcraft.

3. The 16-bit Wars
- Companies: Nintendo (SNES) and Sega (Genesis).
- Pivotal PC Games: Doom (FPS) and Warcraft (RTS).

Section 2: Genre Classification & Evolution

4. Genre Definition and Examples
- Metroidvania: Explore a map, find items to unlock new areas. Example: Castlevania: Symphony of the Night.
- JRPG: Story RPG with turn-based fights. Example: Final Fantasy VII.
- RTS: Real-time strategy with bases. Example: StarCraft.

5. Understanding Hybridization
- Rogue vs. Roguelite:
    - Rogue: Turn-based, lose everything when you die.
    - Roguelite (like Hades): Real-time, keep upgrades after death.

6. The Souls-like Subgenre
- Historical Title: Demon's Souls.
- Core Elements: Hard combat (stamina) and environmental storytelling.

Section 3: Influence and Key Titles

7. Technology as a Driver
- Impact: 3D graphics allowed open worlds and exploration in all directions.
- Key Title: Grand Theft Auto III.

8. Community and Modding
- Process: Players change game files to make new modes.
- Example: MOBA came from the DotA mod for Warcraft III.

9. The Pillars of Gaming
- Title 1: Doom (Fast action/Tech).
- Title 2: Final Fantasy VII (Story/Scale).
- Contrast: One is about visceral action, the other is about emotional cinema.
